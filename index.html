About

WingChunGlossary.net is an attempt to create a comprehensive and understandable glossary of Chinese terms related to Wing Chun and their English meanings. The work is still ongoing. If you encounter any errors or you have suggestions please contact us! Your work will be credited accordingly! This is an entirely free educational resource! No profit is sought after!

Why do we need this?

As a student of foreign languages and practitioner of martial arts for many years it is my opinion that it's very important to exercise some effort in trying to understand the original terminology even if we don't actually learn the language(s) themselves. Such study although seeming difficult on the outside can only help us in the long term and by doing so we might gain insight on what actually we spend so much time on. For a lot of us practicing martial arts searching and learning is a lifelong journey. Terminology can be different between lineages as the natural language changes with time. Some lineages have changed some names entirely. Having a resource such as this can hold an educational as well a historic value.

No seriously, "WHY" do I need this? Chinese is difficult!

Not going to lie, it is difficult if that's your mindset. However, I'd like to ask you to challenge yourself a little bit. Using Chinese characters for the actual terminology is my preferred way to communicate ideas related to Wing Chun when writing? Why you ask? Because they're not ambiguous! Characters represent a very clear idea or meaning(s). Transcriptions are huge source of errors and misunderstandings not only online, but in real-world communication as well. Am I talking about "Gaam Sau", "Gaan Sau", "Gaang Sau"? Do I know exactly what these terms mean? Do you? This website provides the most common Enlgish names and meanings as well as all terms expressed with traditional characters, simplified characters, jyutping transcription and pinyin transcription.

Final word before you dive in

One thing I want to highlight is that all of the actual techniques in Wing Chun are verbs. That means they're actions and not just static shapes. Taan Sau (攤手/摊手) is not just the shape of an 3/4 extended arm with the palm up. Taan Sau is the whole action that led to that final shape at the end. Don't practice rigid shapes! "Be water my friend!"
